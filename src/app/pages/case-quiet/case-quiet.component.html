Introduction

What did I face?

The users

Members (receivers), volunteers, sponsors, and donors with varied tech literacy and languages. They need a calm, accessible app that surfaces the next action, centralizes “geluksmomentjes” vouchers/tickets, and keeps everyone in sync without message overload.

The product

Quiet is a community app for members, volunteers, sponsors, and donors. It centralizes “geluksmomentjes” (vouchers/tickets), events, and messages into one calm place. Role-based dashboards surface the next action, multilingual support lowers barriers, and simple notifications keep everyone aligned without chat overload.

Priorities: clear roles & permissions, resource distribution tracking, donation transparency, and privacy by design.

The business goal

Increase activation and reduce coordination overhead by making Quiet the single, reliable place for tasks, events, and “geluksmomentjes” — integrated into weekly routines for members and volunteers.

How to turn scattered chats and files into one calm, reliable routine for "Quiet"?

MAIN RESEARCH QUESTION

Sub-research questions

  • How do members/volunteers use WhatsApp/SMS/email today—and where do flows break?
  • What single next action should each role see first?
  • Where does the voucher lifecycle (create→allocate→notify→redeem→confirm) fail most?
  • What Organizer tools are the weekly minimum for sponsors/volunteers?
  • What notification rules keep people engaged without noise?

The proposed solution

To decide on the right direction, I surveyed members and volunteers on which concept they found most valuable for their daily routines.
- Members wanted a simple, inclusive app for engagement and navigation.
- Sponsors/Volunteers needed a structured tool to manage and distribute resources.

Concept 1: The Community Connector App

This app serves as a centralized digital hub for community engagement and support, designed to enhance interactions between members, sponsors, and volunteers. It's envisioned as a multi- functional platform that integrates aspects of social networking, event management, and resource sharing tailored to a community-oriented environment. The app should be highly interactive, user- friendly, and supportive of inclusivity, fostering a vibrant community spirit.

Concept 2: The Quiet Navigator App

The Quiet Navigator App is designed as a logistics and operations tool primarily focused on eﬃciently managing the distribution and tracking of "geluksmomentjes." It combines the ease of use seen in consumer apps like Uber Eats with the functional depth of business management tools, tailored to meet the specific needs of Quiet’s operational dynamics.

Concept 3: The Quiet Impact Tracker App

The Quiet Impact Tracker App is focused on visualizing and managing the impact of community activities, donations, and "geluksmomentjes." This app is primarily designed as a tool for tracking and reporting the eﬀects of various initiatives within the Quiet community. It serves as a bridge between members, sponsors, and volunteers, providing real-time data on the contributions and outcomes of the community's eﬀorts, tailored to enhance transparency and encourage more active participation.

Concept 4: The Quiet Organizer App

The Quiet Organizer App is designed to empower sponsors and volunteers with tools to eﬃciently manage and oversee community events and resources. This app serves as a central hub for organizing, scheduling, and tracking, ensuring optimal allocation and usage of resources and facilitating smooth event executions.

What was chosen — and why?

Feedback from interviews and the concept survey made two needs stand out: members wanted a calm place to find what to do next, while sponsors/volunteers needed structure to allocate and track resources. I therefore committed to a two-app approach that works as one system: Community Connector for members and Organizer for sponsors/volunteers.

  • Reduce noise, increase trust: clear “next action” for members; auditable flows for volunteers/sponsors.
  • Meet people where they are: keep WhatsApp/SMS hand-offs, but store decisions and vouchers in Quiet.
  • Privacy by design: role-based access, minimal data exposure between apps.
  • Operational wins first: start with the flows that unblock weekly routines, then add impact reporting.

Members app — Community Connector (Concept 1)

  • Role-based home with a “Next action” banner.
  • Central feed: events, vouchers (*geluksmomentjes*), messages.
  • Simple, respectful notifications; multilingual support.
  • MVP scope: onboarding, interests, voucher wallet, event browse.

Sponsors & Volunteers app — Organizer (Concept 4)

  • Plan and allocate resources; create/assign geluksmomentjes.
  • Track distributions and attendance; lightweight scheduling.
  • Exportable logs for accountability and donor reporting.
  • MVP scope: create voucher/event, assign to member, mark as fulfilled.

What I deferred (next phase)

Impact dashboards and public transparency features from Impact Tracker (Concept 3), and member-to-member social features from Navigator/Connector crossovers, to keep the MVP focused on routine reliability.
