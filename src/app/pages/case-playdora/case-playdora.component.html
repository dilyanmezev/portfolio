Introduction

What did I face?

The users


  • Adults buying meaningful, story-rich gifts.
  • Comfortable with standard e-commerce flows.
  • Value authenticity, craft, and provenance.

The product


  • ~12 unique Ancient-Greek toy figures at launch.
  • “Playful + elegant” brand tone.
  • Owner story and artifact origins are key.

How might we make a playful, elegant online store
that feels like exploring Greek myths — not just “buying”?

MAIN RESEARCH QUESTION

Sub-research questions

  • Story vs shop: do users prefer a myth-led intro or direct product grid?
  • What interaction would lift confidence and conversion?
  • PDP essentials: what info must be visible to choose a figure/variant without hesitation?
  • Mobile performance: what asset/load limits keep AR/images smooth on 4G devices?

Proposed directions

AR 360º

Spin figures in 3D and place in-environment.

Simple game

Mini-game; earn coins and unlock prices.

Color-picker shop

Pick the color of your future toy, price in real-time.

Why chose these for MVP?

AR 360º

  • + Highest “wow” & shareability.
  • + Explains form/finish better than photos.
  • – Heavier assets on mobile if not optimized.

Simple game

  • + Playful brand moment; coin unlocks.
  • + Increases dwell time and recall.
  • – Non-essential to purchase flow.

Color-picker shop

  • + Directly impacts conversion (clarity).
  • + Reduces returns; sets expectations.
  • – Requires consistent photo set per variant.

Outcome

  • Clear, story-led shopping with playful touches (AR/Color picker) ready for MVP.
  • Consistent visuals and component system for fast iteration.
  • Lightweight animations that support, not distract from, decisions.

Handoff includes: component specs, color/typography tokens, and accessibility notes (contrast, focus, keyboard).

Learnings & next steps

  • Show > tell works: large visuals drove clarity in reviews.
  • Variant clarity (color/finish) reduces uncertainty pre-cart.

Next
